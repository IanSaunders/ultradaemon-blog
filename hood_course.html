Hood Hundred Course Details

This 100 mile course offers a world-class scenic tour of Mt. Hood’s high country and its dryer, sunnier eastern backcountry, with great crew access, well-appointed aid stations and exceptional volunteer support. The course format is a lollipop loop-style route—essentially a 20 mile outbound stem from Mt. Hood Meadows to a dynamic 60 mile figure eight loop, then the 20 mile ridgeline return to Mt. Hood Meadows for the finish. This is a true mountain ultra, with a suprisingly fast, runnable first half and a sneaky tough back half. Awe-inspiring views, trails and community support await along this challenging and incredibly diverse course!

Race Timeline

  • Start Time: 5:00AM, Saturday, August 1, 2026

  • On-Course Cutoffs: See Course Chart below

  • Overall Time Limit: 34 hours (3PM at Finish Line on Sunday, July 26th)

Elevation Change

Approximately 17,300’ of elevation gain and 17,300’ of loss.

Course Surfaces

The following surfaces will be encountered. Non-singletrack is intermittent and split into multiple shorter stretches.

  • Singletrack trail: 82 miles, mostly smooth dirt, some segments of rocky and technical

  • Doubletrack (native dirt/rock surface): 8 miles

  • Gravel (maintained forest road): 7 miles

  • Pavement: 3 miles

Aid Stations

The course includes 14 aid stations, with 12 offering full service and 2 being hydration only/self-service (at Gunsight aid station outbound, then again on the return). Review the information and chart below for details:

FULL SERVICE: These 12 aid stations will be staffed with enthusiastic, experienced hosts and volunteers, and amply stocked with the following:

  • HYDRATION: Neversecond C30 sports drink is 30g of carbohydrate and plus 200mg sodium per 500ml (17oz) serving, water, Coke, ginger ale. Coffee and hot water for tea, as well as veggie-based broth also available at most over night and aid stations later in the race

  • FOOD/NUTRITION: Neversecond C30 energy gels in multiple flavors, PB&J sandwiches, cookies, various fresh fruit options, candy, potato chips, gummi snacks, trail mix, and a variety of other ultra fare. Beginning at Aid Station 6, each full service aid station will have some real food options, including multiple savory and/or hot food items such as soup, grilled cheese sandwiches, quesadillas, etc.

LIMITED SERVICE: These 2 aid stations (Gunsight) are unstaffed and self service, stocked with water and Neversecond hydration drink only.

MEDICAL: Each full service aid station will have professional medical personnel monitoring, with a full compliment of first aid and emergency response equipment. 

Cupless Race:

All Daybreak races are CUPLESS. Be sure to carry your own drinking vessel/cup for liquids such as soda at the aid stations.

Required Gear

  • All runners (and pacers) shall have two working headlamps/lights (or other effective illumination devices) and at least one set of extra batteries with them during all hours between 8PM Saturday and 6AM Sunday. No exceptions. Most runners will want to take these items from their Surveyors Aid Station (#6) drop bag, or acquire from their crew at Fifteenmile Aid Station (#7) if arriving before 8PM.

Recommended Gear

Midsummer day time weather is generally mild here, however mountain weather can vary significantly. Night time temps can quickly get cold and sometimes windy after sunset, especially above 5000’. Thunder showers with lightning are fairly uncommon during the summer here, but certainly not out of the question. We expect you to be prepared for a wide variety of weather and temperatures. Recommended supplies include:

  • Clothing layers for cold/windy night time and early morning hours

  • 400 calories / 120 grams of nutrition at all times

  • 30 oz / 1 liter minimum hydration capacity

  • Cell phone or smartwatch with navigation app and predownloaded gpx/kml data file of the course

  • Charger for navigation device/cell phone

Trekking Poles

Trekking poles are allowed.

Trail Etiquette

Many of the course’s trails are popular and used by multiple different groups. Please be courteous to other hikers, runners, and between Bennett Pass Aid Station #2 and #14, mountain bikers and sometimes equestrians. Obviously watch for vehicles on any road/doubletrack segments of the course. If you wish to pass another runner, call out "passing on your left" or "passing on your right" before attempting to pass. Slower runners must yield the trail to runners wishing to pass. Hikers and runners have the right-of-way over mountain bikers, but fast-traveling bikers may not see you in time so always be vigilant.

Equestrians: Horses may be spooked by the sudden appearance of a runner, with potentially serious consequences to everyone nearby. Call out to the rider, stop and step off the trail to let oncoming horses pass. Runners should never pass a horse from behind without first notifying the rider and the rider instructs you when they are ready for you to pass.

Service Requirement

A service requirement is mandatory for all 2026 Hood Hundred race starters and shall be fulfilled between July 1, 2025 and June 30, 2026 and received by Daybreak Racing no later than July 1, 2026. This can be satisfied by performing eight (8) hours of service in any of the following ways:

  1. Organize: Service at any official, permitted ultramarathon or trail race as an organizer/director in any capacity;

  2. Volunteer at a permitted ultramarathon (e.g., working an aid station, sweeping, course marking, etc.);

  3. Trail work (maintenance or new construction) on a trail used by an ultra, under the supervision of a formal trail maintenance organization operating under permit by a land management agency. Examples of the many organizations that meet this criteria in the Pacific NW include Trailkeepers of Oregon, Washington Trails Association, 44 Trails Association, Siskiyou Mountain Club, Cascade Volunteers, NW Trail Alliance, etc.

  4. Donation: If you are unable to meet one of the three criteria above, a minimum $100 donation to a stewardship-focused non-profit organization would meet this service requirement.  Please ensure your donation is made and a copy of the payment confirmation is submitted to us within the required date range/deadline.

TO SUBMIT: The completed Service Requirement Form shall be received by Daybreak Racing no later than July 1, 2026 via email or Google Forms. On July 2, runners who have not turned in their form will be removed from the start list or wait list.

We will work with our beneficiary, 44 Trails, to hold at least one trail work party in the months leading up to the race (likely May and/or June) and would love your participation-- dates and location TBA or posted on their website at https://www.44trails.org.

Special thanks to the Hardrock 100 organizing committee, from whom some of these service requirement criteria were adopted.
